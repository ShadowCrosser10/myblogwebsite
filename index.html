Places you must visit!

New York City

I visited this place in 2015.

New York City is a delightful city bustling with people. It is also the home of two popular tourist attractions: The Statue of Liberty and the Empire State Building. In the evening, you can catch sight of magnificent Brodway performances. And in the night, the glorious Times Square illuminates in front of your eyes, powered by over 30,000 LED's. In addition to all the sights and sounds, New York is a great place to sample morsels like pizzas, fried calmaris, and the ever-so delicate cheescake. With a rich, eccentric history of 400 years, New York is- and will remain- one of the most fantastic cities you can ever visit.

Niagara Falls

I visited this place in 2019.

The Niagara Falls is a spectacular sight to see. It falls on the border between between New York and Canada, with extravengent waterfalls lying on either side. The rush of the plentiful, abundant water makes the water foamy a bit. Yet, it's quite incredible seing all 167 feet of splendor. There are also caves you can go inside that lie behind the waterfall. Overall, I would definetly reccomend you visiting the Niagara Falls, especially for nature afficinados.

Disney World

I visited this place in 2014.

Disney World is an entertainment resort complex located in Orlando, Florida. The firework shows, cruises, and several amusement parks all are part of Disney World's uniquneness. Magic Kingdom is one of Disney World's five divisions. It contains a variety of magic-themed activities, such as Pirates of the Caribbean, Mickey Mouse, etc. The second division is Hollywood Studios, which has rides ranging from Star Wars explorations to a Slinky Dog Dash! Animal Kingdom, Disney Springs, and EPCOT are Disney World's three other divisions, which also have extrodinary features and rides of their own. Disney World is a perfect vacation, and I encourage you to go.

Must-Watch Movies!

Avengers: Endgame

One of the best Marvel Movies ever!

Avengers: Endgame is a fabulous movie, with all of the legendary Avengers we know and love: Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, Hulk, Ant Man, and more. The Avengers are on a quest to revive their fallen allies and defeat Thanos once and for all. The continuation of Avengers: Infinity War, the supervillain in the story is Thanos, an evil demigod who has been decimating the universe and destroying entire planets just for one thing: The Infinity Stones. Endgame and Infinity War are both action-packed, suspenseful, and is truly a treat to watch. I personally love Endgame, and I'm confident that you will too.


Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle

An epic tale of grit and survival

An epic tale of strength and surival, Jumanji is about four high-schoolers getting sucked into a video game called Jumanji. They get an enhanced avatar, new names, and are thrust into the vast jungle of Jumanji, with nowhere to hide and nowhere to go. No matter if it's a pit of poisonous snakes or a herd of stampeding ostriches, the team of "explorers" always manages to escape. This is an excellent movie about grit, determination, and survival, and will definetly be a treat to watch. I recommend this movie immensely.


Avatar

A story about aliens and different planets!

Avatar is a phenomenal movie. The story, set in the year 2154, involves a mission by U. S. Armed Forces to an earth-sized moon in orbit around a massive star. This new world, Pandora, is a rich source of a mineral Earth desperately needs. Pandora represents not even a remote threat to Earth, but we nevertheless send in ex-military mercenaries to attack and conquer them. It's not only the story, but the graphics and advanced tools used to make the movie are incredible as well. Avatar has a compelling story and awesome graphics, which is why I'm reccomending this movie to you today.

News about the environment

  • Exxons' huge oil drilling on Guyana coast poses major environmental threat

    Exxon has been extracting oil from Liza 1, a ultra-deep drilling operation since 2019. By 2025, the company expects to have produced more than 800,000 barrels of oil. Guyana would then be Exxons' largest oil-drilling sight ever. However, experts claim that Exxon is taking advantage of this poor, underdeveloped country and weak government to drill for fossil fuels. They also believe that the drilling is inadequate and dangerous, and the life of locals are at stake from this- something Exxon refuses to believe. "Exxon's time here is temporary", says Vincent Adams, Guyana's former environment chief. "And when they make their billions and go away, we have to clean up the mess they made." The debate to send Exxon away from Guyana and end Liza 1 is still continuing today.

  • California's largest fire torches several homes as blazes move west

    Flames that originiated from the rugged terrain of Northern California destroyed multiple homes. Now, as the fires are moving west, other states and cities are battling the wildfires. The Dixie Fire, (which started on July 14, 2021) has already destroyed a dozen houses and offices when it tore through the community of Indian Falls after dark. An estimated 180,000 acres of land were charred from this, and only 20% of the fire had been contained. Meanwhile, America's largest wildfire, southern Oregon Bootleg fire, was nearly halfway surrounded as more than 2,000 firefighters worked to contain it in the heat and wind. The growth of the sprawling blaze had slowed down, but that doesn't mean it has completely been extinguished. Firefighters will continue to tackle this blaze and attempt to stop it from destroying more, but for now, we should cross our fingers and hope for the best.
